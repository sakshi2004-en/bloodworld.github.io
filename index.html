  • Eligibility Criteria for Blood Donation



    To donate blood, a person must meet certain health and lifestyle requirements to ensure safety for both the donor and recipient. The common conditions include:


    1. Age: 18-65 years (varies by country)
    2.Weight: At least 50 kg (110 lbs)
    3.Hemoglobin Level: Minimum of 12.5 g/dL
    4.Blood Pressure: Within normal limits (typically 100/60 mmHg to 140/90 mmHg)
    5.General Health: Should be in good health with no infections, fever, or recent illness

Terms and Conditions for Blood Request Eligibility for Request

  1. The requestor must be a patient, guardian, or authorized representative.
  2. The patient must have a valid medical requirement for blood transfusion.
  3. A doctor's prescription or hospital request letter is mandatory.
  4. Government-approved ID proof of the patient or requestor is required.
  5. Blood is subject to availability and compatibility with the patient's blood group
  6. The blood bank/hospital is not responsible for adverse reactions post-transfusion.

Here are some fruits that help increase blood level

APPLE

Contains a moderate amount of iron and vitamin C, which help in hemoglobin synthesis and improve iron absorption. Apples contain polyphenols and flavonoids that improve blood circulation and prevent oxidative damage to red blood cells (RBCs).

CARROT

Carrots contain vitamin C, which enhances iron absorption from other foods.Helps in the production of red blood cells (RBCs) and improves overall blood quality.

SPINACH

The green pigment in spinach (chlorophyll) has a similar structure to hemoglobin and may help improve red blood cell count.

DATES

The iron in dates directly contributes to hemoglobin formation, improving oxygen transport in the blood. Copper helps the body absorb iron efficiently and aids in red blood cell production.
